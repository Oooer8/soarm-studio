Troubleshooting

Camera preview fails

On Ubuntu, confirm the camera appears as /dev/video*, keep your user in the video group, and try --backend v4l2. On macOS, grant Camera permission to the terminal application.

Wrong camera role

OpenCV indexes can change. Re-capture previews and re-run setup cameras.

Leader and follower ports are unclear

Do not guess from device order. Return to Hardware setup, run scan --probe-arms before and after hot-plugging each arm, and identify roles from changes in arm_ports.

Serial port denied

On Ubuntu, add the user to dialout, log out and back in, then verify no other process owns /dev/ttyACM* or /dev/ttyUSB*. On macOS, verify the terminal can open /dev/cu.*.

Calibration feels wrong

Re-run one arm at a time. Capture zero carefully, then sweep every joint through the safe range you want available.

Teleop completes but observed_hz is low

The run reached TELEOP_READY, so it did not crash. If observed_hz is far below target_hz, the synchronous Studio loop is spending too long reading leader joints, follower readback, or cameras. At 30 Hz, each loop step must stay under about 33 ms. 

soarm-studio teleop --config configs/session.yaml --free-test --seconds 10 --debug

Compare scheduler_lag, leader_read, follower_before_read, follower_after_read, stream_update, and camera_read:*. OpenCV cameras are read on background latest-frame threads; after this optimization camera_read:* should be small, and camera_age:* shows frame freshness. If every phase is below budget but the observed rate is still low, inspect sleep and scheduler_lag for OS timing drift. If follower_after_read is expensive, leave the default --follower-readback-every 0 in place for live teleop, or sample it occasionally with --follower-readback-every 10.
