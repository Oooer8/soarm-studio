Step 2 Calibration

Calibration first records the physical zero pose, then records each joint's usable range while you move the arm by hand. The program disables torque and reads encoders; it does not command motion.

First complete port and camera binding in Step 1 Hardware setup, then run the readiness check. Calibrate the leader and follower separately, in that order. See Calibration principles for the underlying model.

Calibrate arms

1. Calibrate the leader

The leader uses configs/arms/leader.yaml. After startup, the CLI runs one status check, disables torque, then waits for you to place the zero pose.

CLI 
soarm-studio calibrate --config configs/session.yaml --role leader

When the terminal asks for the zero pose, place every leader joint at the physical zero pose, hold still, then press Enter. Next, slowly sweep the safe usable range of the shoulder, elbow, wrist, and gripper. Do not force the mechanical hard stops. After the sweep, stop the arm and press Enter to write the new zero_tick, direction, and soft limits.

Interactive prompt terminal 
Place every joint in the physical zero pose, then press Enter to record zero ticks:
Freely sweep the full range of motion for each joint. Press Enter when done:

2. Calibrate the follower

The follower uses configs/arms/follower.yaml. After the leader is done, calibrate the follower with the same flow. Each arm must record its own zero pose and dynamic range; do not reuse calibration from the other arm.

CLI 
soarm-studio calibrate --config configs/session.yaml --role follower

If a joint was not moved enough, the output marks it as under-excited. Recalibrate that arm and sweep the affected joint through more of its safe travel.

Output and troubleshooting

The default output is designed for live operation: it shows the current role and interaction steps, then summarizes the config path, zero ticks, sweep ranges, post-check result, and next command. The following examples show successful calibration; ports, ticks, and safe ranges vary with local hardware and arm pose.

On failure, read the summary's recheck and suggestion sections first. For deeper diagnostics, add --debug to see the full report, or add --json to export machine-readable results. Communication failures usually point to the serial port, power, bus wiring, or IDs. Voltage failures should first be checked against the arm's real supply level and the configured low_voltage threshold.

!
Check once more after calibration

After both leader and follower are calibrated, run soarm-studio check --config configs/session.yaml --overwrite to confirm bindings, soft limits, voltage, and cameras before teleoperation.

CLI 
soarm-studio check --config configs/session.yaml --overwrite
